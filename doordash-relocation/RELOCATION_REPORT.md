## 1. The finding that changes the plan: you're already in the top ~10%

The strongest surviving dataset (Gridwise telemetry, **115,771 dashers**, verified
3-0 against the live source): the national median DoorDash driver grosses
**$11.63/hour** (including base, peak pay, tips, promos) at ~1.5 deliveries/hour.
Top-25% cutoff: $13.49/hr. Top-10% cutoff: $15.63/hr.

Your $200–300/day in Colorado — even at $200 over 12 hours — is **$16.67/hr,
above the national top-10% cutoff**. On $300 days you're far above it.

What this means, honestly:
- **Most relocations would be a downgrade.** You're not underperforming your
  market; you're outperforming almost everyone's.
- The claim "even top dashers can't hit $300/day" was itself **refuted** — the
  Gridwise medians include idle time, and a Platinum dasher cherry-picking peak
  hours beats them. You're living proof. But no *verified* data shows any metro
  where $300/day is the expected outcome rather than top-tier performance.
- Your Colorado problem is real (droughts between orders), but it's a
  **consistency** problem, not a rate problem. Solve for consistency.

## 2. Ranked recommendations (verified cost side + access mechanics)

No metro-level earnings data survived verification, so revenue-side rankings would
be dishonest. What survived: taxes, gas, and how market access works. Ranked:

### #1 — Texas: San Antonio / Dallas–Fort Worth / Houston
- **$0 state income tax on your 1099 profit** (Texas's zero tax survived
  verification independently). Whatever Colorado takes from your net profit now,
  Texas takes nothing — an automatic raise with zero extra hours.
- **Gas verified ~$0.37–0.48/gal below national average** (July 2026 AAA: TX ~$3.34
  vs ~$3.82 national). At delivery mileage that's real money.
- Huge, sprawling, car-dependent metros with year-round delivery weather; no
  Colorado winter dead zones.
- Trade-off (verified): Texas has **no gig-worker pay-protection laws** — no
  Seattle-PayUp/NYC-minimum equivalent. Your floor is whatever DoorDash pays.

### #2 — North Carolina: Charlotte / Raleigh
- Flat income tax **cut to 3.99% for 2026** (verified; one of 8 states that cut
  rates Jan 1, 2026), below Colorado's rate, with further trigger-based cuts likely.
- Fast-growing Sunbelt metros, mild winters, lower rent than Denver metro.

### #3 — Stay in Colorado, change tactics (the underrated option)
- You already earn top-decile money. Verified fact: **tips are ~48–49% of dasher
  pay** — and metro Denver tips are not verifiably worse than Sunbelt tips (no
  metro tip data survived). Before paying moving costs, exhaust: different zones
  within CO Springs/Denver/Fort Collins, different dayparts, and the scout-trip
  method below.

**Honorable mentions on cost grounds:** Tennessee/Florida/Nevada metros (no state
income tax — though the full "nine no-tax states" enumeration only went 1-2 in
verification, Texas is the one that survived cleanly). Columbus OH and Oklahoma
City also cut taxes for 2026.

## 3. The single most actionable verified fact: test before you move

Verified market mechanics (3-0): DoorDash **waitlists gate NEW signups only**, per
market. As an **existing active Platinum dasher, you can open the app and dash in
any market in the country** — no waitlist, no reapplication.

So don't relocate on faith. **Run scout trips:** drive to DFW or San Antonio, dash
your normal schedule for 5–7 days, log every shift (orders/hour, $/hour, dead
time), and compare against your Colorado baseline. Two or three week-long trials
cost a few hundred dollars in gas and motels and replace every unverifiable "best
city" blog on the internet with your own data. Only move when a market beats your
Colorado numbers *in your own log*.

(Unverified and worth confirming with support first: whether Platinum *status*
itself carries over across markets — no claim on this survived either way. Your
account works everywhere; the priority-access perk may re-qualify locally.)

## 4. The Tesla verdict: not now, not for this job

All three legs verified 3-0:

1. **The federal EV credit is dead.** New $7,500 / used $4,000 / lease loophole all
   expired September 30, 2025 (P.L. 119-21). No federal purchase incentive exists
   for any EV bought in 2026. (Only a home-charger credit survives to June 2026.)
2. **Used Tesla prices went UP after the credit died** — iSeeCars, 1.7M+ listings:
   Tesla fleet average **+4.3% to $31,329** (Model 3 +2.6%, Model Y +1.3%) while
   **non-Tesla used EVs FELL 3.6% to ~$23,738**. A used Tesla now carries roughly
   a **$7,500 premium** over comparable-age non-Tesla EVs. 2026 is objectively a
   bad entry point.
3. **Delivery-economics guidance ranks cheaper cars above Teslas** for city-route
   gig work: used Chevy Bolt / Kona Electric / Niro EV, or a used **Prius
   (~$0.35/mile all-in)** — with the Model 3 explicitly flagged as "less affordable
   for gig drivers."

**Bottom line:** for delivery, a ~$12–18k used Prius or Bolt beats a ~$31k used
Tesla on every financial axis — purchase price, insurance, depreciation-per-
delivery-mile — and the earnings that would service a Tesla payment are exactly
the earnings you're telling us are inconsistent. If the Tesla is a personal goal,
treat it as a lifestyle purchase to make *after* the income is stable, not as a
delivery tool. (Note: precise EV cents-per-mile charging claims were refuted 0-3,
so the EV-vs-hybrid operating-cost gap is directionally real but quantitatively
unverified — one more reason not to finance $31k on projected savings.)

## 5. The risk nobody's pricing in: you are one deactivation from $0

You said it yourself: Uber Eats and Grubhub are deactivated/inactive. DoorDash is
your **only** income rail, and it can be switched off by an algorithm overnight —
appeal queues take weeks. Before any move, and more important than any move:

1. **Appeal/reactivate the other platforms.** Uber Eats and Grubhub both have
   appeal processes; a reactivated second app is worth more than any relocation.
2. **Add platforms that are still open to you:** Instacart, Walmart Spark, Amazon
   Flex, Shipt. In a drought, a second app converts dead time into orders — which
   is the exact problem you described in Colorado.
3. **Keep 2–3 months of expenses liquid** before relocating anywhere on a
   single-platform income.
4. Guard the account: dash-cam / photo documentation habits, never let completion
   rate ride the deactivation threshold.
